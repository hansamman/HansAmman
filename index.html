About

Hans M. Amman is Professor Emeritus of Economics, internationally recognised for his work in computational economics and for his leadership in higher education governance. His career bridges academic research, institutional leadership, and the development of scholarly infrastructure through journals and professional networks.

He obtained his PhD in Economics from the University of Amsterdam and has held professorial appointments while also serving in senior executive roles at major Dutch universities.

Academic Leadership

University Governance
Vice‑President and Board Member at the University of Amsterdam, Utrecht University, and Eindhoven University of Technology, with responsibility for finance, strategy, and institutional development.

System Leadership
Key contributor to reforms in Dutch higher education governance, accreditation, and research evaluation.

Mentorship & Stewardship
Advocate for academic integrity, interdisciplinary research, and sustainable university management.

Scholarly & Institutional Impact

Beyond his own publications, Hans Amman has shaped the field by founding and editing journals that defined computational economics as a discipline.

  • Founding Editor‑in‑Chief, Computational Economics
  • Founding Editor, Computational Management Science
  • Editor, Netnomics and New Dimensions in Networks

His work is widely cited and continues to influence research methodology, policy modelling, and interdisciplinary economic analysis.
